# Storytelling Frameworks

## And-But-Therefore (ABT)

The most versatile framework for technical content.

- **AND** — establish context and status quo
- **BUT** — introduce the conflict/challenge
- **THEREFORE** — present the solution and resolution

**Two patterns:**
- Good AND Good, BUT Bad, THEREFORE… (performance updates, QBRs)
- Bad AND Bad, BUT Good, THEREFORE… (turnaround stories, incident postmortems)

**Example:**
> "Our platform handles 1,015 vendor submissions AND scales across 3 host cities, BUT traditional forms produce completion rates below 30%, THEREFORE we deployed a conversational AI agent that validates data at the point of collection."

## Problem-Solution-Impact (PSI)

Gold standard for case studies — 93% of top SaaS companies use this structure.

1. **Problem** — customer pain with specifics (metrics, consequences)
2. **Solution** — connect features directly to pain points
3. **Impact** — quantified results with before/after comparison

**Always include:** Baseline metric → post-metric → timeframe (all three).

## 1-3-1 Framework

Distills complexity into focused message. Effective for quarterly reviews, dashboards, board presentations.

1. **One** core idea
2. **Three** pieces of evidence
3. **One** clear takeaway or recommendation

## Pixar Story Spine

Six-sentence format adapted for B2B:

1. "Once upon a time…" — your customer's world
2. "Every day…" — their daily struggle/status quo
3. "Until one day…" — discovering your solution
4. "Because of that…" — first transformation
5. "Because of that…" — cascading benefits
6. "Until finally…" — new reality with measurable outcomes

## Data-to-Story Arc

1. **Context** — establish baseline and what was normal
2. **Tension/Change** — what shifted or broke
3. **Resolution** — evidence-based action

**Example opener:** "We're processing 2,678 vendor submissions — and I think I know why the old way broke."

## So-What Framework

Prevents data-dumping. Every insight must connect to business impact.

1. **What happened** — the observation
2. **Why it matters** — the significance
3. **What to do next** — the action

## McKinsey Pyramid Principle

Top-down communication. Lead with conclusions, support with 2-4 key insights, reinforce each with data.

## Case Study Best Practices

### Structure
1. **Headline** — client name + product + quantified result
2. **Snapshot box** — industry, size, region, products, key KPIs
3. **Challenge** — pain with metrics and consequences
4. **Solution** — features mapped to pain points
5. **Results** — before/after table (4 rows minimum)
6. **Quote** — direct customer testimonial
7. **CTA** — clear next step

### Numbers to Include
- Baseline → post-metric → timeframe (always all three)
- Time-to-value (implementation time to impact)
- A dollar figure if permitted
- Revenue growth %, time savings, cost reductions, efficiency gains

### Market Validation Framing
- 53% of B2B buyers say case studies make it easier to evaluate shortlisted solutions
- 49% of SaaS marketers rate case studies as the #1 tactic for increasing sales
- Filter case studies by industry vertical so decision-makers find comparable scenarios
- Use direct customer voice, never paraphrased testimonials
- Include realistic timelines and challenges — authenticity builds trust
