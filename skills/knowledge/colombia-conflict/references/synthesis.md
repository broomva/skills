# Hay Futuro Si Hay Verdad — Consolidated Synthesis

**The Final Report of the Colombian Truth Commission (CEV), 2022 — digested whole.**

> *Comisión para el Esclarecimiento de la Verdad, la Convivencia y la No Repetición.* 11 tomos / 24 books / ~8,900 pages / ~3.67M words, read and consolidated. President: **Francisco José de Roux**. Mandated by the 2016 Havana Peace Accord (Decreto 588 de 2017). This file is the apex synthesis; per-volume digests live in `digests/`.

---

## 0. What this is — in one breath

After a 60-plus-year internal armed conflict that produced **~9 million victims (≈90% civilians)**, the 2016 Peace Accord between the Colombian State and the FARC-EP created three bodies — the **JEP** (special peace tribunal, individual criminal responsibility), the **UBPD** (search unit for the disappeared), and the **CEV** (Truth Commission, *collective* ethical/political/historical responsibility) — together the **Sistema Integral para la Paz**. In 3.5 years the CEV heard **~30,000 people across ~14,000 interviews** in 28 *Casas de la Verdad* and **24 countries**, took 1,000+ civil-society reports, and produced this report. Its thesis is its title: **there is a future only if there is truth** — and its single demand is **No Matar** (Do Not Kill) and *sacar las armas de la política* (take weapons out of politics).

**The Commission's own framing of its uniqueness:** unlike most truth commissions, there was **no preceding dictatorship** to indict, a continuous formal democracy and the *garantista* 1991 Constitution remained in place, and the violence **continues** — because the structural causes were never resolved. It is a truth commission writing *during* the war it describes.

---

## 1. The headline findings (what the Commission officially concluded)

1. **A prolonged, intolerable, normalized human catastrophe** — one of the longest humanitarian crises on the planet — met with **indifference from large sectors of society** (*negacionismo*).
2. **No right to war, but a real armed conflict** — IHL applied; *even war has limits* (*"hasta la guerra tiene límites"*); all armed actors committed war crimes and crimes against humanity against civilians.
3. **Differentiated, graduated responsibility** — *"a mayor poder, mayor responsabilidad."* The State, as guarantor of rights, bears the heaviest duty; it **amplified** the catastrophe through its **alliance with paramilitarism**.
4. **The "falsos positivos" are a crime of State** — thousands of civilians (JEP: **at least 6,402**, concentrated **2002–2008**) executed and falsely presented as combat kills; the mothers' cry *"¿Quién dio la orden?"* (Who gave the order?).
5. **Narcotrafficking is a central protagonist and persistence engine** — and so is the **war on drugs** itself; prohibitionism is a *driver*, not a cure.
6. **Impunity is structural** and is itself a cause of the war's recycling.
7. **A democracy wounded by war** — the persistence of *armas en la política*, the *enemigo interno* doctrine, and the political genocide of the **Unión Patriótica** (≈5,000+ killed).
8. **The campesinado was the principal victim; ethnic peoples bore the war most destructively; the economic model of exclusion and land concentration must change or the war will recur.**
9. **The war is not over.** The same actors remain the principal responsibles across every statistical estimation; victims are still being produced (315+ FARC ex-combatants and 1,327+ social leaders killed *after* the Accord).

## 2. The numbers — the statistical universe of the conflict

The backbone is the **Proyecto conjunto JEP-CEV-HRDAG** (statistician **Patrick Ball / HRDAG**): integration + deduplication + statistical estimation (capture-recapture / multiple-systems estimation) across **112 databases from 42 institutions**. Crucially, it treats **subregistro (under-counting) as a measurable object**, not an excuse.

| Violation | Documented | Period | Estimated real universe |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Total victims** | **~9,000,000** (≈90% civilian) | 1985–2021 | — |
| Homicides / deaths | **450,664** | 1985–2018 | ~**800,000** |
| Forced disappearance | **121,768** | 1985–2016 | ~**210,000** (searchers cite 110,000+) |
| Kidnapping | **50,770** | 1990–2018 | ~**80,000** |
| Recruitment of minors | **16,238** (≈44% under 15) | 1990–2017 | ~**30,000** |
| Forced displacement | **7,752,964** (RUV: >8,000,000) | 1985–2019 | — (the report's "8 million") |
| Exile | **>1,000,000** | 1982–2020 | ~2× prior estimates; RUV recognizes <2% |

**Supporting magnitudes:** selective assassinations **~178,000**; **massacres 4,237** (≈24,600 dead, peak year 2001); sexual violence (RUV) **32,446** (≈92% women/girls — massively under-reported); antipersonnel mines ~**9,900** victims; confinement **>351,000**; land dispossessed **>8,000,000 hectares**.

**The concentration claim (load-bearing):** roughly **1996–2010 produced ~75% of registered victims**; massacres peaked 1998–2002; *falsos positivos* peaked 2006–2008 — the violence peaked as the war "de-escalated," not when it began.

## 3. Why the war happened and persisted (the causal model)

The Commission rejects single causes: the war is an **entramado** (interwoven web) of political, economic, institutional, military and narco interests. The deep "geological faults" (Molano) of nation-building:

- **Land / the agrarian question** — the unbroken thread. Failed reforms (buried by the 1972 *Pacto de Chicoral*), a **violent counter-agrarian reform**, **8M ha dispossessed**, peasants pushed onto the *frontera agraria* instead of redistributing fertile land. Colombia is among the **10 most unequal countries on earth** in land/income.
- **Restricted democracy + political exclusion** — the *Frente Nacional* elite pact closed the system; the recurring cycle of "opening democracy by gunfire" and its violent sabotage.
- **The Cold War's *enemigo interno*** — a mirror doctrine (the State's "internal enemy," the insurgency's "class enemy") that converts the differing other into an enemy to be eliminated; imported via the 1962 **Yarborough mission** and the Escuela de las Américas.
- **Weak/selective State** — *"más territorio que Estado"*: the State present as coercion, absent as guarantor of rights; it **legalized civilian arming** (Decreto 3398/1965 → Ley 48/1968 → Convivir) which evolved into paramilitarism.
- **Narcotrafficking** — the *comodín* (wildcard) that "kicked over the board," financing all armed armies, buying politics (*parapolítica*), laundering through land. The guerrilla entered narco for finance; narcos entered the war to protect business.
- **Impunity** — the structural enabler that lets the cycle repeat.
- **The economic model + third-party complicity** — extreme inequality, under-taxation of wealth, *terceros civiles* (economic actors) who **paid paramilitaries** to displace and dispossess for agro-industry, mining and land. A database of **258 economic actors / 439 business actors sanctioned** in Justicia y Paz sentences.

**The cultural diagnosis: "modo guerra"** — a cognitive/cultural condition in which Colombians, even unarmed, cannot conceive of the other thinking differently, and turn opponents into enemies. Reconciliation requires *desinstalar las narrativas de odio*.

## 4. The armed actors and responsibility attribution

Terminology is deliberate: State/paramilitary acts = **violaciones de derechos humanos**; guerrilla acts = **abusos / infracciones al DIH**. Responsibility is **collective (ethical-political), not individual-criminal** (that is the JEP's job).

**Lethal-victimization ordering:** most non-combatant dead were killed **first by paramilitaries, then guerrillas, then State forces.**

| Actor | Origin → trajectory | Principal responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Paramilitaries (AUC)** | Civilian-arming decrees → MAS (1981, *hito fundacional*) → Puerto Boyacá model → Casa Castaño/ACCU → Convivir → AUC (1997) → demobilized 2005, recycled into successor groups | **Homicide & forced disappearance (largest share, ~45% / ~52%)**; massacres; the cruelest sexual violence |
| **FARC-EP** | Communist *autodefensas campesinas* → Marquetalia (1964 founding myth) → Conferencias VII/VIII militarized & narco-financed → signed 2016 Accord | **Kidnapping (~40%, principal) & child recruitment (~75%, principal)**; mines; Bojayá |
| **State / Fuerza Pública** | Built "for war" (Cold War doctrine); the *cuarto poder* | **Falsos positivos (6,402)**; responsibility *for paramilitarism* (origin, training, alliance "at least until 2005"); homicide ~12%, disappearance ~8% by direct action |
| **ELN** | Founded 1964 (Simacota), *foquista* + liberation-theology (Camilo Torres); never demobilized | Kidnapping (~19%); oil-pipeline extortion; mines |
| **EPL / M-19** | Maoist (1967) / urban-nationalist (1970, stolen election) | Both largely demobilized 1990–91 into the Constituyente |
| **Narcos & *terceros civiles*** | Medellín & Cali cartels; economic/political elites | War financing, land laundering, *parapolítica*, killing of unionists |

## 5. How the war was fought — patterns & mechanisms

Six **motivational dynamics** behind the violations (from the violations volume): (1) sustaining/disciplining the armed actor; (2) social control of civilians; (3) reconfiguring territory for economic ends; (4) physical/symbolic elimination of "enemies"; (5) obstructing peace; (6) annihilating the adversary in open breach of IHL.

Mechanisms: **stigmatization** as the device that "facilitates, justifies and endorses" attack (the *enemigo interno*); **the anti-euphemism doctrine** ("no repetition begins by calling things by their name" — rejecting "errores," "daños colaterales," "falsos positivos"); body-count incentives ("llenar un tablero"); listas + encapuchados for massacres; *vaciamiento estratégico* (strategic emptying of territory). Most events **chained multiple violations** together.

## 6. The territorial logic — geography is the key (*Colombia adentro*, 14 books)

The war is **legible only as geography**: *"el conflicto no se puede pensar si se sustrae al mapa de su territorio."* Its spatial grammar:

- **Dualidad territorial / "más territorio que Estado"** — an integrated core vs. abandoned extractive peripheries (*el revés de la nación*). The State is present as security force, absent as rights-guarantor.
- **Violence tracked accumulation** — each regional war organized around a commodity: **banana + gold** (Antioquia), **cattle + palm + coal** (Caribe), **emeralds + the agrarian question** (Centro), **coffee-crisis + narco-laundering** (Eje Cafetero), **oil + coca + the border** (Frontera Nororiental), **oil royalties** (Orinoquía), **coca + ports** (Nariño/Pacífico), **sugar + narco + ports** (Valle/Cauca).
- **Paramilitarism as a franchise** — invented in **Magdalena Medio** (the "Puerto Boyacá model": cattle ranchers + narcos + Army + politicians), exported coast-to-border.
- **The city is a battlefield, not a refuge** — *urbanización de la guerra*: intra-urban displacement, *fronteras invisibles*, *limpieza social*, Comuna 13 / Operación Orión.
- **The campesinado was the principal victim** (45.5% of victimizing acts) **and a denied political subject** — its erasure as a *sujeto político de derechos* is itself a finding.
- **State *connivencia*** (complicity, *nóminas paralelas*, *"dejar hacer, dejar pasar"*) recurs in nearly every region.

## 7. Who bore the war — the differential volumes

The report's most distinctive structural choice: dedicated volumes for the populations the war struck disproportionately.

- **Ethnic peoples — *Resistir no es aguantar.*** ~13.6% of population but **23.7% are registered victims**. The conflict is the latest layer of a **contínuum de violencias** rooted in *trato colonial* and *racismo estructural*. Harm is **collective, territorial, spiritual** — *cuerpo-territorio*, *territorio como víctima* (Decreto 4633), *etnocidio*, peoples (Awá, Nukak) at risk of **physical & cultural extinction** (Auto 004/2009). Emblematic: **Bojayá, Bahía Portete, Naya, Cacarica**. Resistance: *guardia indígena/cimarrona*, the *bastón de mando*, *consulta previa*, autonomy. *"Resistir no es aguantar"* — to resist is not to endure.
- **Women & LGBTIQ+ — *Mi cuerpo es la verdad.*** The body as *territorio de guerra* and *botín de guerra*; **patriarchy and war reinforce each other**. 15 modalities of sexual violence; **reproductive violence** (forced contraception/abortion *inside guerrilla ranks*); LGBTIQ+ persecution as *"limpieza social"* and *violencias por prejuicio* to impose a heteronormative order. Resistance: *juntanza*, the Havana *Subcomisión de Género*, *"no queremos ser pactadas, sino pactantes."*
- **Children & adolescents — *No es un mal menor.*** The title rebukes the framing of child-harm as a "lesser evil." Recruitment was **"naturalized and intentional."** Documented minors: homicide **64,084**, displacement **3,049,527**, recruitment **16,238** (FARC-EP ~75%; 44% under 15 = a war crime). **Operación Berlín** — the Army knowingly killed surrendering minor recruits.
- **Exile — *La Colombia fuera de Colombia.*** **The first truth commission to systematically include the diaspora** (testimony in 24 countries via a *nodos* model). **>1M exiled**, RUV recognizes <2%; exile is an **invisibilized grave violation** in itself — *"no son víctimas que están fuera, sino que tuvieron que salir."* Coined: *insilio* (inner exile of those who stayed), *destierro*, *destiempo*.
- **Impacts & testimony — *Sufrir la guerra y rehacer la vida* + *Cuando los pájaros no cantaban.*** *Daño* defined morally as a **deliberately produced desequilibrio**; **trauma biocultural** (people + social fabric + land injured together); **nature/territory as a rights-bearing victim**. But also **afrontamientos y resistencias** (coping vs. transforming) — Colombia built **memory *before* the war ended** (Doris Salcedo's *Fragmentos*, costureros, Bojayá's alabaoras). The testimonial volume's lesson: a society that cannot **listen in depth** to its *"voces de los nadies"* will recycle the war.

## 8. The recommendations — the architecture of *no repetición*

**67 recommendations in 8 thematic blocks**, each with an addressee and horizon; monitored by a *Comité de Seguimiento*. Estimated reparation need: **COP 328.3 trillion**. The blocks:

1. **Build peace as a national project** — full Accord implementation; negotiated exit with the **ELN**; dismantle paramilitary successors; **create a Ministerio para la Paz**; restore relations with Venezuela.
2. **Integral reparation, memory & dignity** — State recognition of responsibility in *falsos positivos*, paramilitarism, and the UP genocide; permanent **Museo de Memoria**; strengthen the **UBPD**.
3. **Inclusive, deliberative democracy** — political/electoral reform; protect social protest; **officials must stop stigmatizing** leaders/defenders; recognize the *campesinado* as a subject of rights.
4. **Confront narcotrafficking & drug policy** — shift **from the drug war to strict legal regulation** (*regulación legal estricta*); treat use as public health; support *campesinos cocaleros*; reform international drug policy.
5. **Overcome impunity** — independent **Fiscal General** selection; prioritize systematic-violence & corruption investigation; condition extradition on prior clarification for victims.
6. **A new vision of security (most detailed)** — **human security** over the war/enemy & drug-war paradigm; transform the security sector under **civilian democratic control**; reform intelligence archives (derogate the 30-year reserve); control private security & firearms.
7. **Territorial peace** — agrarian reform reversing extreme land concentration; multipurpose cadastre; constitute/expand ethnic territories; ratify the **Escazú Agreement**; accelerate PDET & land restitution.
8. **A culture to live in peace** — education for peace; peace journalism; faith communities; and **continue the Commission's legacy** (the Report, the Human-Rights Archive, the Transmedia).

## 10. Cross-cutting meta-insights (the "so what")

1. **Methodologically pioneering.** The JEP-CEV-HRDAG estimation project is one of the largest human-rights data efforts in the world; it makes **under-counting itself a quantified finding**. And the exile volume is the **first systematic diaspora truth process** anywhere.
2. **Truth as a *public good* and a *public event* (*acontecimiento de la verdad*)** — evidenced equally by victims' courage and by the *negacionismo*, fake news, and attacks the Commission itself faced. The report does **not** claim to own the truth ("le pertenece a la verdad").
3. **An expanded ontology of victim and witness** — not only persons but the **social fabric, the territory, and nature** are victims; not only words but bodies, photographs, poems, even rivers and birds are testimony.
4. **The differential architecture is the message** — by giving ethnic peoples, women/LGBTIQ+, children, exiles, and territories their *own* volumes, the report argues that a single national narrative would itself reproduce the erasure that enabled the war.
5. **The hardest finding: the war is not history.** Written in the present tense — *"esta guerra está viva"* — because the structural causes (land, exclusion, narco, impunity, *armas en la política*) remain. Hence the entire report is **forward-pointing**: the recommendations, not the catalogue of horror, are its telos.
6. **The wager.** *Hay futuro si hay verdad* — recognition (not vengeance) is what heals victims, transforms perpetrators, and makes the catastrophe structurally unrepeatable. *"No es con la venganza, sino con los ideales y el amor."*
