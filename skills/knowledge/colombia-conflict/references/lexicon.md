# Lexicon — the coined / load-bearing concepts of the CEV report

The report introduced (or load-bears) a precise vocabulary. Knowing these terms
is the fastest way to reason with the report's framing. Machine-readable source:
`data/concepts.json` (query with `cc.py concept --search <term>`).

| Term | Gloss | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| **paz grande** | The "great peace" — a national, deliberative peace that opens democracy, removes weapons from politics and confronts structural causes (not just ending the State–FARC-EP war). | Convocatoria |
| **no repetición** | Non-repetition — the telos of every recommendation: make the catastrophe structurally unrepeatable. | Hallazgos |
| **modo guerra** | "War mode" — the cultural condition in which one cannot conceive of the other thinking differently, turning opponents into enemies. | Convocatoria |
| **enemigo interno** | "Internal enemy" — the symmetrical Cold War doctrine (right and left) making the differing other an enemy. | No matarás |
| **falsos positivos** | Civilians murdered by the Army and presented as combat kills (≥6,402, 2002–2008). *"¿Quién dio la orden?"* | Hasta la guerra |
| **hasta la guerra tiene límites** | "Even war has limits" — IHL binds all parties; *no hay derecho a la guerra*. | Hasta la guerra |
| **a mayor poder, mayor responsabilidad** | Graduated responsibility — the State, as guarantor of rights, bears the heaviest duty. | Hasta la guerra |
| **terceros civiles** | "Civilian third parties" — economic/political actors who financed/determined violence; a fourth axis of responsibility. | Hasta la guerra |
| **entramado** | The war as an interwoven web of interests, not a clash of armed apparatuses. | No matarás |
| **más territorio que Estado** | The selective/absent State: present as coercion, absent as rights-guarantor (*dualidad territorial*, *el revés de la nación*). | Colombia adentro |
| **contrarreforma agraria** | Violent reversal of agrarian reform via dispossession, leader assassination and narco land accumulation. | Colombia adentro |
| **reprimarización** | Re-primarized export economy (oil/minerals/coca) coincident with armed-group growth. | Colombia adentro |
| **urbanización de la guerra** | The city as battlefield: intra-urban displacement, *fronteras invisibles*, *limpieza social*. | Colombia adentro |
| **negación del campesinado** | Erasure of the peasantry as a political subject of rights — itself a finding. | Colombia adentro |
| **contínuum de violencias** | The conflict as the latest layer of historical violences (colonial racism; patriarchy). | Resistir / Mi cuerpo |
| **racismo estructural** | Structural racism armed actors assimilated and replicated against ethnic peoples. | Resistir |
| **cuerpo-territorio** | "Body-territory" — harm to a body is harm to the land; *territorio como víctima* (Decreto 4633). | Resistir / Mi cuerpo |
| **etnocidio** | Ethnocide — peoples (Awá, Nukak) at risk of physical/cultural extinction (Auto 004/2009). | Resistir |
| **pervivencia** | Continued existence/survival of a people — what resistance defends. | Resistir |
| **daño como desequilibrio** | Harm as a deliberately produced disequilibrium (Thiebaut), described via destroyed forms of life. | Sufrir la guerra |
| **trauma biocultural** | Integrated injury to people + social fabric + land, by armed AND "development" actors. | Sufrir la guerra |
| **genocidio político / politicidio** | For the Unión Patriótica extermination (~5,000+ killed). | Sufrir / Hallazgos |
| **afrontamientos vs resistencias** | Coping (survive/manage) vs. resistance (confront/transform); *resistir no es aguantar*. | Sufrir / Resistir |
| **juntanza** | Collective solidarity — women rebuilding the social fabric (*no pactadas, sino pactantes*). | Mi cuerpo |
| **cuerpo, primer territorio de paz** | The affirmative LGBTIQ+ resistance frame. | Mi cuerpo |
| **masculinidades guerreras** | War-rewarded "real man" across all armed actors; the report urges demilitarizing masculinity. | Mi cuerpo |
| **violencia reproductiva** | Violation of reproductive autonomy (forced contraception/abortion in ranks; forced pregnancy). | Mi cuerpo |
| **violencias por prejuicio** | Anti-LGBTIQ+ persecution to impose a heteronormative order (not "homophobia"). | Mi cuerpo |
| **limpieza social** | "Social cleansing" of those deemed "undesirable", often announced in *panfletos*. | Mi cuerpo / Colombia adentro |
| **no es un mal menor** | "It is not a lesser evil" — child-harm was targeted, not collateral. | No es un mal menor |
| **vinculación** | "Linkage" of minors = *reclutamiento* (joining ranks) + *utilización* (exploitation); "naturalized and intentional". | No es un mal menor |
| **la Colombia fuera de Colombia** | The exiled population as a still-belonging part of the nation (*tuvieron que salir*). | La Colombia fuera de Colombia |
| **insilio** | "Inner exile" of those who stayed behind, having lost something fundamental. | La Colombia fuera de Colombia |
| **destierro / destiempo** | "Banishment / un-earthing" and "out-of-time" — exile as life uprooted and a limbo that never ends. | La Colombia fuera de Colombia |
| **guardia indígena / cimarrona** | Unarmed ethnic self-protection bodies bearing the *bastón de mando*; rooted in *cimarronaje*. | Resistir |
| **consulta previa** | "Prior consultation" (ILO 169 / Ley 21 of 1991) — also the method by which the ethnic volume was built. | Resistir |
| **Puerto Boyacá model** | The exportable paramilitary franchise (ranchers + narcos + Army + politicians); *Convivir*, *parapolítica*, *Pacto de Ralito*, *connivencia*. | Colombia adentro |
| **acontecimiento de la verdad** | "The event of truth" — truth as a public good and happening; the Commission does not own the truth. | Convocatoria |
| **vaciamiento estratégico** | "Strategic emptying" — depopulation to free land for agribusiness/extraction; dispossession as product, not byproduct. | Colombia adentro / Hasta la guerra |
| **seguridad humana** | "Human security" — the recommended paradigm, replacing the war/enemy and drug-war frame. | Hallazgos |
