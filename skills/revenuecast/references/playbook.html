01

The thesis, and the one hard correction

The pattern is real. It almost always gets sold wrong — and the wrong version is precisely what feeds the 88%.

Thesis. Define a distinctive brand + aesthetic → use gen-AI to produce high-volume content showcasing the output of a capability → the content is the advertisement (desirability + accessibility-via-AI = demand) → monetize by selling the system that reproduces it for someone else's brand.

The correction that makes it true · review · HIGH

The scarce, non-leakable input is not the method. It is the SHOW step — distribution + taste + a running engine. Prompts are one API call away through any aggregator; quality leadership rotates quarterly. The graveyard is full of people who bought the method and never built the show.

The binding rule

Kleos sells the self-demonstrating ENGINE that produces the SHOW — never "the method" generically. The prompts are not the moat; they leak in days. The proof and the product are the same artifact: a copycat without the engine cannot fake the output.

The engine must pick ≥1 defensible layer. kleos_check.py FAILs any manifest whose moat.primary is prompts or templates.

Non-negotiable

Prompts and templates are NOT a moat. They leak in days. Anything you can paste into a DM is not defensible. Pick from the grid below — and if your "edge" isn't on it, you don't have one.

04

The offer ladder — a validated wedge, not a 6-tier deck

Designing the closed-execution and license-recursion tiers before T1 converts a single dollar is info-product self-flattery. Build the solid wedge first. Earn the rest with measured numbers.

v1 — the two-step wedge (the only thing you build first)

T0 · Free showcase + lead magnet ($0) — the high-volume on-brand output IS the ad; a free mini-kit (one template + the 4-layer skeleton + the retention-threshold card) delivered via the capture rail → email, the owned asset no algorithm can throttle. kleos_check.py requires an owned_channel.

T1 · Tripwire ($17–29 one-time) — 40–60 prompts = a full workflow + the model-routing cheat-sheet + the sequencing order + caption/hook vault. Synthetic personas only (likeness firewall, §5). The first dollar that converts viewer → buyer.

T-spine · One recurring community ($39–99/mo, push annual) — the durable spine: recency re-teaching + member showcase + the client-getting module. Recurring out-earns one-time by ~89%. This is the asset that compounds.

Earned — design only after measured conversion

T3 Paid challenge ($49–97) — manufactures public proof. T5 VIP / done-with-you / closed-execution ($497–7.5K) — LTV ceiling + moat protection. T6 Affiliate / license-recursion (20–29%) — the outermost loop; the literal Kleos recursion (sell the engine to people who run it for their vertical). None of these gets a slide before T1 converts.

The deterministic gate — what "done" means

A Kleos engine instance is real iff kleos_check.py kleos.manifest.yaml passes all seven gates. Reasoning doesn't validate; a passing checker does.

G1Identity present. capability, brand.name, brand.gloss all present.
G2Validated wedge. offer_ladder has ≥1 free + ≥1 paid + ≥1 recurring tier.
G3Own the audience. distribute.owned_channel present.
G4Real moat. moat.primary ∈ {sequencing, recency, community, agency-proof, compliance, closed-execution} — never prompts/templates.
G5Survival. governance has disclosure_labeling, likeness_firewall, earnings_claims_substantiated, spend_cap, platform_diversification (≥3 + owned).
G6Ritual-vs-Substance. self_improvement.claimed ⟹ mechanism_built && measured, else FAIL.
G7KPI honesty. kpis.imported block present whenever benchmark numbers appear.
The binding line

A capability that doesn't pass all gates is not a revenue engine — just content that happens to exist today.

The Ritual-vs-Substance gate, made concrete

The draft claimed self-improvement was a fork of symphony-egri/batch.rs. The reviewer read the file: it computes one scalar — completed/(completed+retrying) — and increments a pass/fail tally. It is not a variant selector. Real self-improvement (multi-armed bandit + per-variant retention attribution + brand-DNA fold-back) is unbuilt NEW code.

Binding rule · G6

No Kleos surface — buyer-facing OR internal — may claim "self-improving" / "the loop rewrites tomorrow's content" unless the mechanism is built AND measured. All conversion benchmarks (comment-DM 25–55% trigger, 70–80% challenge completion, $1,800 LTV, 235× CAC) are imported from the consumer AI-celebrity niche and unvalidated for any other buyer — especially the high-trust technical (B2D) buyer. Presenting imported numbers as validated targets is a slop tell and a real FTC risk.

Why the agentic-dev wedge (L2) is the strongest niche

The market named the problem in 2026: "harness engineering" (Hashimoto → OpenAI / Lopopolo, Feb 11 2026 → Thoughtworks / Böckeler: a literal "cybernetic governor combining feed-forward and feedback to regulate the codebase") — which is Carlos's RCS control-metalayer, described two layers shallower.

Generation is commoditized; governance is the scarce input. Cursor FastRender (Jan 2026) proved ~2,000 agents write 3M+ lines/week — and The Register + SIG confirmed "shoddy code at scale." The wedge: making a swarm shippable — safe, reviewed, self-improving, un-sue-able. The slop reversal inverts into an advantage: when consumers distrust AI volume, "it actually shipped and passed CI" is an authenticity signal volume-slop cannot manufacture.

The single Day-1 cornerstone bet: run a small bstack-governed multi-agent build of one real feature; publish the measured quality delta (CI pass-rate, review-catch count, LOC churn) vs an ungoverned swarm. One numbers-backed artifact > 20 stylized terminal Shorts — and platforms structurally cannot reproduce it.
